About the SARS-Cov-2 virus
The current pandemic is caused by the SARS-Cov-2 virus. In the majority of the cases, this virus causes mild pneumonia, known as lung inflammation. However, in severe cases, this virus can cause symptoms such as shortness of breath and low blood oxygen saturation. In the worst scenario, this virus could lead to respiratory failure, septic shock (caused by low blood pressure) and even the failure or dysfunctioning of organs.
This virus is part of a family of Coronaviruses of which several other viruses are known to cause diseases in humans. Some of them only cause mild symptoms, but the virae SARS-Cov and MERS-Cov are both known to also cause deadly lung inflammation. The first originated in China in 2003, the latter in Saudi Arabic in 2012.

Killing the virus

To kill the SARS-Cov-2 virus, multiple approaches can be taken. Like every virus, SARS-Cov-2 is sensible toÂ heat. Although relatively stable, research indeed shows SARS-Cov-2 can be inactivated with heat treatment. Another method is UV-radiation. Since SARS-Cov-2 is very sensible to UVC-light (part of the UV-light spectrum), UV-treatment could be another way to kill this virus.
