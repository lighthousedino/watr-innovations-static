Our journey

Follow our journey of the development of WATR3.0. From preliminary research to ideation to the final prototype, the design went through numerous iterations and extensive research.
style reference (DO NOT DELETE)
September
The design challenge

The project A Design Challenge in Times of COVID-19 started in the DesignLab on the campus of the University of Twente. There, we chose our first topic, the Global South.



We chose the global south because we believe that people there need a solution more than people in western countries do. Less developed countries are also severely affected by the pandemic, but often don't have the resources to follow basic guidelines and impose measures.

See our initial challenge
Milestone 1 Digital story

For milestone one we created a digital story, in the form of a two minute video, introducing our challenge, objective and stakeholders.



Whilst making this video, we chose to only focus on water provision. Water is essential in fighting the virus. However, the human right of having access to water is often not complied with. Therefore, we want to address this problem, because it will help during the pandemic but also beyond.

Watch the video
Milestone 2 Design concept poster

Making a design-concept poster, was our task for milestone two. It had to include the first iteration of our context study, containing, a research question, visualisation of our stakeholders, keywords and capabilities, context and existing technologies.



To narrow down, we all did research in different African countries. Out of this research, we got to Ethiopia, because in Ethiopia there is a huge lack of water and clean water. Its in second place on the WHO list of countries with worst water conditions. Furthermore we also narrowed down our topic. During a meeting we dropped agriculture and decided to conduct further research into cleaning water, water management, sanitation services and water sourcing and accessibility in Ethiopia. The biggest problem seemed to be clean water, especially in the rural areas.

October
Deliverable A

Deliverable A was the first full iteration of our context study, the first step in the personal deepenings and a team reflection.



For this deliverable, in terms of the problem, we narrowed down the last bit. From Ethiopian rural areas, we narrowed down to the rural Ethiopian lowlands, because the problems in the lowlands are more severe.
Read the report
Milestone 3 The script of our design

In phase 2, milestone three, we started to look more towards a solution, especially what a possible solution should do and how it should be responsible, appropriate and suitable. In order to show this, we created a visualisation of our stakeholders, their needs and through these the requirements and functions of our solution. This was assisted by a text with more background information on norms, policies and possible interactions.



From now on, we started to focus on a solution. During an extensive brainstorming session, we decided on what we think our solution should do, being, cleaning and storing water for a large group of people. By deciding this, we ruled out many possible solutions, but also opened the gate to specifying exactly what our solution should look like and what it will do, but also, what we still have to consider and think about.

The road to milestone 3
November
Deliverable B

Deliverable B is the place where almost everything comes together. Most of the decisions are made. The context study is done, the visualisation is iterated, storyboards are made and five different prototypes for the final product are included. Moreso, a more detailed iteration of the separate deepenings is included.



For deliverable B the main thing to do, was to come up with five different prototypes. In order to do so, we came up with lists of different parts of our product, for which we could have different iterations. We came up with different options for the shapes, taps, inflows/getting to inflow height, insides, recycling systems and filters. We made five unique combinations, our five prototypes.

December
The next step

Right now we are in the last stage. The main task for now is synthesizing everything into short sections for our website and deciding what our final product was going to be.



For deciding the final product, we got together and all wrote down which features we like best in all different sections. We mostly agreed after which we went back to the drawing table to sketch out the best positioning of all the elements of our solution.
