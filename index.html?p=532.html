The Ethiopian lowlands

Ethiopia

Ethiopia has not only one of the fastest growing economies in Africa but is also characterized by a rich history and cultural diversity. Since the discovery of ‘Lucy’ (oldest human skeleton), Ethiopia is often referred to as the cradle of humankind. It’s porous geography with many valleys and canyons as well as the multitude of rivers, give the country an imposing nature.
However, while its economy is growing, Ethiopia still struggles with major shortcomings in the infrastructure. Especially a severe lack of water supply systems and hygiene services leads to major health issues and high child mortality.

Education

The overall literacy level is 51%. However, 15–24-year-olds have a way higher literacy rate (70%) then older adults (15%).

The lowlands
Ethiopia can be divided in the plateaus of the highlands, which rise in the middle of the country and are characterized by mild climate and many rivers/lakes, and  the surrounding lowlands. Between those two regions a big inequality exists regarding access to basic infrastructure and economic status, hence the way of living.
Ethiopia can be divided in the plateaus of the highlands, which rise in the middle of the country and are characterized by mild climate and many rivers/lakes, and  the surrounding lowlands. Between those two regions a big inequality exists regarding access to basic infrastructure and economic status, hence the way of living.
Especially the rural Ethiopian lowlands suffer from a substantial shortcoming of WASH (water, sanitation, and hygiene) services. Therefore our product attempts to bring maximum functionality and ease of use to the local inhabitants in those regions.

Water resources

Accessible water resources differ significantly within the lowlands. While some communities have direct access to rivers/lakes, others have to rely on the rare rainfall and wells. Wells have been a common solution to make water accessible in Ethiopia.

However, since the groundwater is mostly salty and brackish, as well as due to a lack of ground water mapping it is difficult to build sustainable and most importantly safe wells without additional purification.

Daily life

Living in areas such as the lowlands often means walking for hours every day to get water, getting sick because of the contained contaminants, no time for school, not healthcare, physical labor and relying on external parties to get enough goods to survive.

The social life of people in the Lowlands is mainly shaped by small communities, islamic values, their clan and the traditional task division between sexes. Due to a focus on livestock, many have a nomadic lifestyle moving to the best grazing areas. However, the combination of various livelyhoods is getting more and more common.
