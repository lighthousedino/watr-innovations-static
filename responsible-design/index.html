Responsible design

A core value we have held close to our heart, throughout the design development process, is a strong sense of responsibility for our product and its impact.
Designers’ responsibility
We believe in order to create an ethical, appropriate, accessible, and sustainable product, we must focus on people peoples experiences and values over technological advancement. We created WATR3.0 with the goal of helping people in the Ethiopian lowlands. In that regard, the awareness of potential societal impact is the first step to creating a responsible design.
According to the theory of technological mediation proposed by philosopher PeterPaul Verbeek, technological artefacts have the power to influence and shape human behaviour and perception of the world, and hence carry ethical implications. WATR3.0 sits in between end users (human) and Ethiopian lowlands (world) and mediates their behaviour and experiences by providing access to clean water.
With this in mind, we, as designers, have the responsibility of inscribing morality into our product via appropriate design to make sure the product creates positive changes to the people of Ethiopian lowlands, our end users, within their context. For example, WATR3.0 was designed to be portable so that it is appropriate for the end users who lead a nomadic lifestyle.
Enhancing capabilities
It is not what you have but rather what you can do that is, your capabilities. The formulation of our design requirements stemmed from the capability approach, an economic and philosophical framework that revolves around the idea that possession of a set of capabilities not physical goods or wealth is required to achieve well-being. We designed WATR3.0 with the goal of enhancing the following capabilities:

Life

To be able to live to the end of human life of normal length; not dying prematurely.

Bodily health

To be able to have good health, adequately nourished, and sheltered.

Imagination & thought

To be able to imagine, think, and reason, create self‑expressive work freely.

Play

To be able to laugh, to play, to enjoy recreational activities

We strongly believe in inclusivity and accessibility. Our goal is to make WATR3.0 accessible for as many people as possible. It has to be usable in every region of the Ethiopian lowlands independent of age, gender, and ethnicity. In addition to the four intended capabilities, inclusivity is therefore part of our core values for responsible design development.

Local solution; global movement
The development of WATR3.0 supports the UN Sustainable Development Goal 6 for clean water and sanitation which is supported by the local Ethiopian government through the One WASH National Programme (OWNP II). The programme stipulates guidelines and policies for development and efficient use of water resources to guarantee access to safe and sustainable water sources for everyone. Specific to the lowlands, the programme specifies that more diverse technologies are needed to fully utilise rain and surface water as opposed to only springs and wells.
